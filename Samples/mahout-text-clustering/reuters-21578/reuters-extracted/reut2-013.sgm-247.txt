INDONESIAN AIRLINER CRASH KILLS 27 PEOPLE

Twenty-seven people were killed and 18 injured when an Indonesian airliner flew into electricity lines and tumbled to the ground in an explosive ball of fire, airport officials said.     The Garuda Indonesia Airways DC-9 crashed yesterday on a domestic flight while carrying 37 passengers and eight crew members, an airline spokesman said.     The plane, made by McDonnell Douglas Corp <MD.N>, cost 18 mln dlrs and was one of 19 DC-9s in Garuda's fleet of 74 jets.  REUTER &#3;

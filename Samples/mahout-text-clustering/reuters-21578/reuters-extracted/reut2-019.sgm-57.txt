JAPAN LIFE INSURERS WARY OF U.S. BOND INVESTMENT

The chairman of the Life Insurers Association told a press conference that life insurance companies  will increasingly diversify their investments away from U.S. Bonds to protect themselves against currency swings, an association spokesman said.     Terumichi Tsuchida said Japanese investment in U.S. Bonds is decreasing, but not drastically,     Life insurers are increasingly placing some of their funds in other currencies, including bonds in Canadian dlrs and in European currencies, he told the conference.

SIX REPORTED WOUNDED IN ECUADOR GENERAL STRIKE

At least six persons, including three soldiers, were reported wounded at the start of a general strike in Ecuador called to press the government to suspend an earthquake-related austerity programme.     The information ministry said workers had partially complied with the strike. Witnesses said bus transport ground to a halt in Quito and schools were closed throughout the country, but banks and shops remained open.     Three soldiers on a military truck were burned by a gasoline bomb, information minister Marco Lara told reuters.     In the central city of Latacunga, three workers were wounded in clashes with police, unionists and hospital authorities said. The strike was called for one day but could be extended.  Reuter &#3;

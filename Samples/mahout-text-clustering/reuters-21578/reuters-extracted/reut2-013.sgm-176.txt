venezuelan bank begins signing debt contracts

the central bank began signing contracts by which it will provide dollars at a preferential rate for the payment of 7.8 billion dlrs of foreign debt, under a new plan announced last december.     A statement from the bank said twelve companies have already been called to sign the agreements and the process is expected to continue in the coming weeks.     Under the new eight-year repayment plan, private sector debtors may obtain dollars at the preferential 7.50 bolivar rate by paying a premium of of 3 bolivars on principal and up to 1.50 bolivars on interest.

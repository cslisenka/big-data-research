TALKS BEGIN TO END BOLIVIAN MINERS' STRIKE

Union leaders of 9,000 miners employed by the state corporation Comibol began talks with the government today on ways on ways to solve their strike over pay, a mining ministry spokesman said.     Labour minister, Alfredo Franco, met with Juan Lechin Oquendo, the veteran secretary general of the Bolivian Labour Organization (cob) and Victor Reyes, the miners federation top leader, at the mining ministry, he said.     "This time we shall start negotiations but continue with our moves to press the government until a solution is found for our demands," Lechin told reporters shortly before the talks.  Reuter &#3;

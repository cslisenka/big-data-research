WALL STREET STOCKS HIGHER IN LATE MORNING

Wall Street stocks staged a strong rebound after being hurt by an early round of futures related sell programs and profit taking. The advance was led by automakers and a firm oil sector. Special situation stocks Texaco and UAL were actively traded.     The Dow Jones Industrial Average, down more than 10 points in the first minutes of trading, stood at 2415, up 10 late this morning. Advances led declines by only a slight margin on volume of 77 mln shares. GM gained two to 83-1/2 and Ford one to 91-1/8. Chrysler rose 1/4 to 58-1/2. Texaco gained 3/8 to 34 and UAL fell 5/8 to 65-1/8.  Reuter &#3;

U.S. LAWMAKERS SAY STOCK DIVE CALLS FOR SUMMIT

Two congressmen urged President Reagan to to call congressional leaders to a domestic economic summit to settle budget and trade matters they said contributed to the stock market fall.     Noting the nosediving New York Stock Exchange, Rep David Obey, a Wisconsin Democrat and former chairman of the Congressional Economic Committee, said "for the last five years this economic recovery has been running on borrowed money and borrowed time."     In another House speech, Democrat Donald Pease of Ohio said a domestic economic summit was "desperately needed."  Reuter &#3;

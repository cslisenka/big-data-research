GEORGIAN AVALANCHES AND FLOODS BRING DEATHS

Avalanches, landslides and floods have killed almost 100 people since the start of the year in the Soviet Transcaucasian republic of Georgia, the official daily Sotsialisticheskaya Industriya said.     Some 16,000 people have been left homeless and 80,000 hectares of arable land and plantations rendered useless, the newspaper said. The figures are still preliminary, but it estimated total losses at over 300 mln roubles.     It said a large number of livestock and birds were killed in the natural disaster and more than 1,800 km of roads needed repairing.  REUTER &#3;
